## Professionally

- I kept working on **Saturday Quiz Time**, learned a lot and had fun
- My **Legis Music** contract ended, but I'm proud of what I achieved in a year
- I earned money from Open-Source for the first time, by working on Astro as a **core maintainer** 
- I started working for **Seibert Group** at the end of the year, with some unique challenges
- I took more holidays, but still less than I should. Area of improvement for 2025!

## Open-Source

- At the end of 2023, I decided to invest more time and energy in Astro. I think it showed:
- I created [astro-integration-kit](https://github.com/florian-lefebvre/astro-integration-kit) to help build integrations. It's now one of the most Astro related downloaded NPM packages
- I created [astro-integration-template](https://github.com/florian-lefebvre/astro-integration-template) which helped people ship dozens of integrations this year (Improvements coming soon!)
- I made many integrations and packages, including [astro-loading-indicator](https://github.com/florian-lefebvre/astro-loading-indicator) and [astro-typed-links](https://github.com/florian-lefebvre/astro-typed-links)
- I found out maintaining all of those is hard, and calmed down a bit
- Astrolicious and astro-tips
- I championed the [`astro:env`](https://docs.astro.build/en/guides/environment-variables/#type-safe-environment-variables) RFC and contributed this big feature to Astro, which was released as stable in 5.0
- I became an Astro core maintainer and helped maintaing the framework
- I met some of the Astro core team at the Dublin off-site

## Personal life

- I moved back to France from Valencia (Spain)
- I improved my English level thanks to the many Astro calls
- I started running
